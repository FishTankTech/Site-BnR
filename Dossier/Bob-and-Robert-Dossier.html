Introduction

Bob & Robert: The Cremean Archives (henceforth referred to as "The Archives") is a fine art estate cataloging and digital preservation project centered on the estates of the late Robert "Bob" Cremean and Robert De Lavergne.

In Total, the two created a collection of over 3,500 specific installation-style pieces, over 200 pieces of pottery, over 100 traditional paintings, over 1,500 pages of original hand-drawn sketchwork and sketchbooks, and over 1,000 published pages of literature focused on the body of work.

With a career spanning nearly 70 years, there was not much left untouched in the art scene by Cremean. "Bob" Cremean was a sculptor, educator, art enthusiast, and an overall steward of an art form for nearly his entire life.

Whether it was through his 1954 Fulbright Scholarship in Rome, his years teaching, or the decades of solo and group exhibitions that followed—the Venice Biennale, the Whitney Biennial, and the Smithsonian Institution, to name a few.

Cremean's work found permanent homes in major institutions including the Los Angeles County Museum of Art, the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the Crocker Art Museum, and the National Collection of Fine Arts in Washington, D.C.

"The Archives" exist as a tool to document, digitize, and responsibly steward this collection—building a complete photographic and archival record of each piece, researching provenance and artist history, and identifying appropriate long-term homes for the physical works, including museums, galleries, and public collections.

Current Physical Holdings

While the archival record and curated documentation preserve the full scope of Cremean and De Lavergne's combined body of work, only a handful of physical pieces remain in our physical collection today.

Over the years, the estate's holdings have gradually been dispersed, sold, and distributed—an outcome common to large, career-spanning estates like this one.

The pieces that do remain, however, carry outsized significance: they represent the last physical remnants of a substantial American art career, and ensuring their long-term preservation and public accessibility is the project's primary goal.

It is the hope of "The Archive" that these remaining works can find a permanent home in a public institution—through sale or outright donation— where they can be preserved, studied, and made available for exhibition or rotation, rather than remain outside institutional care.

A small number of physical works remain in the collection and are available for sale or donation. Full details, dimensions, and inquiry links for each piece live on the project's digital archive, The Digital Estate. Although several entries are still being finalized for the online archive.

Partnership Opportunities

We are currently exploring opportunities to place portions of the collection with institutions that can preserve, interpret, and share these works with the public.

Areas of Interest

  • Acquisition of individual works, curated groups, or the collection in whole or in part.
  • Consultation regarding how the collection may complement your institution's existing collections, mission, or interpretive focus.
  • An introductory discussion to better understand your institution's collecting priorities, acquisition process, and potential areas of mutual interest.

We would be pleased to provide high-resolution images, a complete inventory, and additional provenance documentation upon request.

Whether your institution is actively considering an acquisition or is simply interested in learning more about the collection, we welcome the opportunity to continue the conversation.
