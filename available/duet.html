Synopsis

Robert Cremean and Robert de la Vergne met and became life partners in the early spring of 1963. “Studies For A Double Portrait” marks the fiftieth anniversary of that meeting.

Across five decades the artist describes the two of them as having transformed into what he called the “brown-eyed / blue-eyed monster of cyclopean conceit.” Images and inscriptions across the sculpture record elements of their life together: joys shared, cultural pressures endured, and the private mythology formed between them.
